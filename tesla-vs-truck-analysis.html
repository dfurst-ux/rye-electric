Key Points for Finance

✓ Replace a $1,200/mo expense with a $484/mo fixed liability. The company gas card is a variable, open-ended operating expense with no ceiling. The loan payment is fixed at $484/mo for exactly 60 months, then gone. Net cash flow improvement from Day 1: $716/month.
✓ Operating expense converted to a depreciating asset. Loan payments build equity in a company-funded vehicle. Gas card spend returns nothing — it is pure expense with zero residual value.
✓ $7,500 federal tax credit (IRS Form 8936) reduces net acquisition cost to $28,500. Down payment required: $2,850. Amount financed: $25,650 at 5% APR over 60 months. Total interest paid over loan term: ~$3,380.
✓ Fuel expense line goes to $0 permanently. Employee's home Supercharger covers all charging costs. No reimbursement, no gas card, no fuel receipts to process. Accounts payable workload on this employee's vehicle drops to maintenance only.
✓ 5-year direct cost savings: $44,710. Even accounting for the full loan cost, the Tesla saves the company $8,942/yr over the truck during the loan period. After payoff, savings increase to $14,750/yr in perpetuity.
⏱ Productivity recovery adds $137,500/yr in labor value. 30 hrs/week currently lost to manual driving × $88.16/hr fully loaded rate × 52 weeks. Tesla FSD converts drive time to billable/productive hours. This value is not speculative — it is recovered capacity from an already-compensated employee.
⏱ ROI: vehicle net cost recovered in under 3 months. $28,500 net purchase price ÷ $11,461/mo in recovered productivity = 2.5 months to full payback. 5-year combined financial benefit (cost savings + productivity): $731,960.
